The substrates are the rules for reading it.  
Binary data is replaced by **generative measurement**, enabling minimal storage and maximal expressive area.

\[
z = x \cdot y
\]

Dimensional Programming replaces:

- objects  
- states  
- stored data  
- lookup tables  
- meshes  
- textures  
- frames  

…with **substrates** that interpret the manifold on demand.

## **4. Dimensional Inheritance**
Dimensional Programming uses a 7‑stage inheritance ladder:

1. **Point**  
2. **Length**  
3. **Width (length rotated 90°)**  
4. **Height (length rotated again)**  
5. **Plane**  
6. **Volume**  
7. **Whole (collapsed to a point in the next dimension)**  

Each dimension:

- contains all before it  
- sees the next as a point  
- is created by a 90° rotation  
- preserves one coordinate  

This is the **orthogonality rule**.

The manifold supports **all angles**, not just orthogonal ones.

# 📜 **DIMENSIONAL PROGRAMMING CONSTITUTION**  
### *The Laws, Axioms, and Operational Rules of the Paradigm*

## **Preamble**
Dimensional Programming is founded on the principle that **all computation emerges from geometry**, and that **all geometry emerges from a single manifold**.  
Substrates interpret; the manifold exists.

This constitution defines the laws governing dimensional computation.

---

## **Article I — The Manifold**
1. There shall be **one manifold**, defined as:  
   \[
   z = x \cdot y
   \]
2. The manifold is the **source of all geometry**.  
3. The manifold shall not be duplicated, subdivided, or replaced.  
4. All dimensional structures shall be derived from this manifold.

---

## **Article II — Substrates**
1. Substrates are **rules for measuring the manifold**.  
2. Substrates shall not store geometry.  
3. Substrates shall interpret geometry **on demand**.  
4. Substrates may be composed but not mutated.  
5. Substrates define:
   - rotation  
   - traversal  
   - sampling  
   - dimensional interpretation  
   - projection  

---

## **Article III — Dimensional Inheritance**
1. Dimensions shall follow the 7‑stage ladder:
   - point  
   - length  
   - width  
   - height  
   - plane  
   - volume  
   - whole  
2. Each dimension contains all before it.  
3. Each dimension sees the next as a point.  
4. A new dimension is created only by a **90° rotation**.  
5. Fractional rotations produce **fractional dimensions**.

---

## **Article IV — Orthogonality**
1. A 90° rotation preserves one coordinate.  
2. Anything less than 90° slides through points.  
3. Sliding through points produces friction.  
4. Orthogonality is the only frictionless state.

---

## **Article V — Efficiency**
1. The system shall store **rules**, not **results**.  
2. Binary data shall be replaced by **substrate interpretation**.  
3. The manifold shall be the only geometric storage.  
4. All angles, shapes, helices, spirals, and Schwarz Diamond states shall be generated from the manifold.

---

## **Article VI — Determinism**
1. All transformations shall be deterministic.  
2. All recursion shall be explicit and bounded.  
3. No implicit loops shall exist.  
4. All reasoning shall maintain dimensional coherence.

---

## **Article VII — Emergence**
1. All objects, functions, and structures are **manifold projections**.  
2. Nothing shall be stored that can be generated.  
3. Nothing shall be duplicated that can be derived.  
4. Nothing shall be represented twice.
